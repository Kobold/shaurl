Why use SHA-1 Us?

Mostly because it steals good ideas from git. When you enter a URL it is SHA-1 hashed to create the "shortened" URL. If you use the SHA-1 Us service, you can cryptographically verify (with a reasonable degree of assurance) that you were redirected to the correct URL by just hashing the URL and comparing it with the hash on the end of the shortened url. Users don't have to worry about malicious URL manipulation.

The second good idea I steal from git is that you can use whatever portion of the URL is convenient for you. If the full "shortened" URL you receive is http://sha1.us/9c17e047f58f9220a7008d4f18152fee4d111d14 (way too long), then feel free to use http://sha1.us/9c17e047f58f9220 or even just http://sha1.us/9c17e. Be aware that the probability of collisions increases if you use a shorter portion of the hash.
